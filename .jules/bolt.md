## 2024-05-03 - [Fast single-pass homo_lumo search]
**Learning:** `process_omol25.py` frequently runs over hundreds of orbital occupancies during raw data parsing. List comprehensions allocating arrays with multiple passes were visibly slow for this operation. A single backwards/forwards pass tracking values without allocating intermediary lists yields ~50-60% speedup on these parsing operations.
**Action:** When extracting boundaries (like max index or first occurrence) from long primitive lists, always prefer single-pass tracking loops over array-allocating comprehensions in high-frequency parsing functions.
